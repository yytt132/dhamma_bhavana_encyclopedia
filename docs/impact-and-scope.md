# Impact and Scope

## The Gap

Korean-speaking practitioners have access to Buddhist institutions, teachers, dictionaries, canonical archives, and many printed resources. Yet there is still no open, modern, practice-oriented encyclopedia comparable in usefulness to the English-language ecosystem around SEP, DDB, Princeton, SuttaCentral, and 84000.

This matters because many practice terms are difficult to understand through translation alone. Terms such as `bhavana`, `sati`, `samatha`, `vipassana`, `samadhi`, and `dhyana` are not simple equivalents of modern English or Korean words.

## Intended Users

- Korean-speaking Buddhist practitioners.
- Meditation instructors.
- Dharma teachers.
- Serious beginners.
- Translators.
- Digital humanities researchers.
- Open-source contributors interested in contemplative knowledge infrastructure.

## Public Benefit

The project aims to provide:

- Clear Korean explanations of practice concepts.
- Tradition-aware distinctions.
- Source-aware metadata.
- Human-reviewed AI-assisted drafts.
- Open schemas for reuse.
- A future website and search interface.

## Long-Term Scale

Estimated public content targets:

- 100 MVP entries.
- 300 core SEP-style essays.
- 400-500 glossary entries.
- 100-170 comparison essays.
- Public knowledge graph export.
