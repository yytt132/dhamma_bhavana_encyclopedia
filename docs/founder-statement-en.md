# Founder and Maintainer Statement

This document records the background of the founding maintainer of **Dhamma Bhavana Encyclopedia** and explains why this project was started.

## Practice and Teaching Background

The founding maintainer is based in Korea and works as a Buddhist meditation instructor and meditation instructor.

The maintainer has practiced meditation for about ten years. This project grows out of that practice background, not only from an abstract interest in data or software. It responds to a practical problem encountered by Korean-speaking practitioners and meditation instructors: reliable, open, practice-oriented explanations of Buddhist meditation concepts are difficult to find in Korean.

## Buddhist Study Background

The maintainer studies Korean Buddhism, Southern/Theravada Buddhism, and Tibetan Buddhism.

This multi-tradition background is important for the project. The encyclopedia should not explain Buddhist practice from only one sectarian or doctrinal perspective. Practice terms often shift meaning across traditions, texts, lineages, and modern contexts. Terms such as `samatha`, `vipassana`, `sati`, `dhyana`, `samadhi`, and "mindfulness" need careful distinction rather than simple translation.

For that reason, this project treats each concept as source-aware and context-dependent. A term should be explained through language, tradition, textual background, method, goal, and practice context.

## Translation Work

The founding maintainer is also working on Korean translation projects for the Taisho Buddhist canon, the Pali canon, and the Tibetan Buddhist canon.

This translation work is deeply connected to the encyclopedia. To explain practice concepts well, it is necessary to connect original texts, translation choices, dictionary entries, doctrinal interpretation, practice instructions, and modern usage. When these materials remain scattered, Korean-speaking practitioners have difficulty seeing the whole picture.

**Dhamma Bhavana Encyclopedia** is an attempt to organize this work into a public, reviewable, open-source knowledge base.

## Why This Project Was Started

English-speaking students of Buddhism and contemplative practice have access to strong reference ecosystems: the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, the Digital Dictionary of Buddhism, the Princeton Dictionary of Buddhism, SuttaCentral, 84000, and other scholarly or practice-oriented resources.

Korean-speaking practitioners do have Buddhist dictionaries and reference works, but there is still no sufficiently open, reliable, practice-oriented encyclopedia for meditators, meditation instructors, dharma teachers, and serious beginners.

This project exists to help fill that gap.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

As founding maintainer, the maintainer is responsible for:

- Setting project direction.
- Defining the editorial standards for a Korean practice encyclopedia.
- Maintaining source-aware ontology and schemas.
- Preventing publication of copyright-risk material.
- Maintaining Korean and English documentation.
- Preserving distinctions across Buddhist traditions.
- Keeping AI-assisted drafts under human review.
- Preparing long-term integration with Dhamma Reader.

This project is not intended to turn one person's interpretation into authority. It is intended to create an open, reviewable structure where Korean-language Buddhist and contemplative practice knowledge can be built collaboratively.
