# Mission Statement

This project exists to build an open practice encyclopedia for Korean-speaking Buddhist and contemplative practitioners.

English-speaking students of Buddhism and contemplative practice already have access to a rich reference ecosystem. There are SEP-style philosophical resources, high-quality Buddhist dictionaries such as the Digital Dictionary of Buddhism, specialist reference works such as the Princeton Dictionary of Buddhism, and textual or translation platforms such as SuttaCentral and 84000.

Korean-speaking practitioners do have important Buddhist dictionaries, reference works, and canonical archives. But there is still no sufficiently open, reliable, practice-oriented encyclopedia for meditators, meditation instructors, dharma teachers, serious beginners, and Korean readers who want to understand Buddhist practice in depth.

This gap matters.

Many people need explanations that are accurate but accessible: explanations that distinguish original terms from translations, separate traditions rather than flatten them, connect concepts to actual practice contexts, and make clear when a claim is doctrinal, scholarly, practical, clinical, popular, or uncertain.

**Dhamma Bhavana Encyclopedia** is an open-source project created to help fill that gap.

We aim to:

- Build reliable Korean-language explanations for Buddhist and contemplative practice concepts.
- Provide foundational material for practitioners, meditation instructors, and serious beginners.
- Explain practice terms through tradition, language, textual background, method, goal, and lived practice context.
- Carefully distinguish different concepts that are often hidden under the same translation.
- Combine SEP-style essays, Buddhist dictionary-style lexical entries, practice ontology, and source-aware annotation.
- Use AI as a research and drafting assistant while keeping final interpretation and review under human editorial responsibility.

This project is not just an attempt to collect more information. It is an attempt to build a clearer, deeper, safer path for people who study and practice in Korean.
